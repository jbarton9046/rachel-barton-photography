Andrews Bald, Tennessee

High above Gatlinburg, the trail opens to a broad, wind-brushed meadow where the light hits different. Our adventure sessions are built around movement and laughter—less posing, more story—so the pictures feel like the day itself.

We started before dawn, coffee steaming in the cool air, and reached the ridge as the sky softened from slate to peach. The grass leaned with the wind, clouds slipped along the valleys, and the Smokies did what they always do—turned every small moment into something cinematic. We walked, we laughed, we warmed our hands, then let the morning unfold: twirls in the tall grass, a quiet pause facing the mountains, and the kind of frames you only get when the sun breaks the horizon.

Field notes

Best light

Sunrise or late golden hour keeps the mountains soft and the meadow glowing. Morning usually brings calmer trails and fresher air.

Move-friendly wardrobe

Layers and textures photograph beautifully in the wind. Comfortable shoes and hands-free bags keep us nimble on the trail.

Weather = mood

Fog, sun, clouds—every scenario tells a different story here. We’ll watch the forecast and adjust as needed for the best experience.
